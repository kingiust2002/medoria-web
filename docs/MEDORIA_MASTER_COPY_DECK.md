# Medoria Health + Beauty — Master Copy Deck

Status: ready for localization and implementation  
Scope: public-facing words only; preserve routes, layout, taxonomy, database and business logic  
Canonical language: English  
Target locales: `tg`, `ru`, `en`, `fa`

## 1. Strategic direction

### Medoria Health

Voice: clinically calm, precise, useful and procurement-aware. Lead with the buyer's task. Prefer clear nouns and short verbs over broad superlatives.

Positioning line:

> Professional medical supply, made easier to navigate.

### Medoria Beauty

Voice: quiet confidence, selective, modern and professional. Luxury should be felt through restraint and specificity, not repeated as a claim.

Positioning line:

> A more considered world of professional beauty.

### Shared language rules

- Say what the visitor can do: explore, compare, request, discuss, confirm.
- Keep headlines short. One idea per sentence.
- Use `professional` and `considered` more often than `premium` or `luxury`.
- Do not use `world-class`, `best`, `leading`, `trusted`, `verified`, `authentic`, `certified`, `official`, `exclusive` or `guaranteed` unless Medoria has written evidence.
- Do not promise response time, delivery time, stock accuracy, pricing advantage, market coverage, samples, invoices or contracts unless the owner confirms the capability.
- Do not make medical efficacy, safety, treatment or diagnostic claims.
- Product-specific claims must come from an approved manufacturer source, never from generic site copy.

## 2. Remove or replace before launch

The current site contains claims or placeholders that should not remain public without evidence:

- `500+ products`, `1000+ products`, `24/7`, `Fast delivery`, `Verified supplier`
- `No middlemen`, `prices below market`, `no hidden markups`
- `Stock and prices update in real time`
- `Reply within an hour`, including contact and procurement sections
- `Delivery across Tajikistan`, unless operationally confirmed
- `All sizes available`
- `Only from verified suppliers`, `genuine products`, `every shipment inspected`
- `Aligned with ISO & CE requirements`
- `Replacement on any discrepancy`
- all placeholder brand names such as MediCare, SafeWear, LumiSkin and Rose Atelier
- all current Health testimonials and named customer identities unless written permission and evidence exist
- statements that real warehouse, product or team photography already represents Medoria

Until the owner supplies proof, the copy below replaces those claims with clear, useful and defensible language.

# 6. Localization instructions for Opus 4.8

Claude must localize the canonical English copy into `tg`, `ru` and `fa` with these rules:

1. Preserve meaning, evidence level and CTA intent exactly.
2. Do not add claims that are absent from the English source.
3. Tajik is the primary public locale; write natural Tajik Cyrillic, not Russian sentence structure with replaced words.
4. Russian should sound like professional B2B catalog language used by clinics and distributors.
5. Persian should be concise, modern and neutral; avoid slang and excessive Arabic formality.
6. Keep `Medoria`, `Medoria Health`, `Medoria Beauty`, `WhatsApp`, `Telegram`, `SKU`, `B2B`, `ISO` and `CE` in their approved Latin forms. ISO/CE may appear only in actual product documentation, not generic brand claims.
7. Do not translate brand or product names stored in the catalog.
8. Keep headings visually close to the English length. If a translation is longer, shorten it without changing the claim.
9. Use one consistent term for catalog, quotation, availability, professional inquiry and minimum order in each locale.
10. Validate RTL punctuation, numeral direction and form placeholders in Persian.
